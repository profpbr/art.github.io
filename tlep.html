APPLIED RESEARCH TECHNIQUES

Course Code: 25MCOM1H02   |   Semester: First   |   Credits: 4

Teaching–Learning–Evaluation Plan (TLEP)

Course Objectives To gain a comprehensive understanding of the research process, applying various approaches, methods, and tools to design, conduct, and present high-quality research studies, while upholding research ethics and effective communication standards.

Course Outcomes (CO)

CO No. Course Outcome Bloom’s Level (BTL)
CO1 Explain the meaning, purpose, and scope of research, and distinguish between different research approaches and processes. 2
CO2 Formulate hypotheses and design sampling plans, selecting appropriate sampling methods. 3
CO3 Design effective data collection methods, including constructing scales and questionnaires. 3
CO4 Prepare and analyze data using various statistical tools. 4
CO5 Prepare well-structured research reports, adhering to ethical standards and responsible research practices. 4

Module-wise Teaching Plan

Module Topic No. of Hours
1 Foundations of Research – Meaning, Purpose, Scope, Research approaches, Research process, Literature review, Research design. 12
2 Hypothesis Construction and Sampling – Hypothesis role and formulation, Sampling concepts and methods. 12
3 Data Collection and Measurement – Scaling, Questionnaire design, Data sources. 12
4 Data Processing and Analysis – Data validation, editing, statistical analysis (Factor, Regression, Discriminant, Conjoint). 12
5 Report Writing and Research Ethics – Report structure, citations (APA, MLA, etc.), plagiarism, ICT in research. 12
